Conclusion: Time Frame

An ideal time to launch the crew to Mars is 2033-2035, as this is the point where Mars and Earth are closest. Utilising cycler orbits with conjunction class trajectories enables the shortest trip to Mars and back. This reduces costs, planning and medical risks to the crew. This requires all the necessary technologies to be developed and ready in 12 years, including:

  • Establishing equipment in a low Earth orbit
  • Sending equipment to Mars in advance
  • Creating a safe transfer vehicle for the crew.
  • Sourcing the multiple rockets required to transport equipment and fuel the crewed vehicle

If this time frame were to be taken seriously, the mission process would need to start immediately and quickly utilise collaborations with other agencies, especially the private sector.

While it is not essential to launch between 2033-2035, it would make the most sense to get the crew there by 2040. It should be noted that this is a very ambitious time frame due to the intense amount of infrastructure required before the crewed mission even takes place.
