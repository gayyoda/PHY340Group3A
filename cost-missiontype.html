Cost by Mission Type

Mars Semi-Direct

NASA estimated the original Design Reference Mission (which used the Mars Semi-Direct mission) to cost $55 billion for 3 missions over 10 years [33]. Despite being an estimate, the DRM is an incredibly well researched and realistic document, made by NASA themselves. However, since the first report is from 1993, the reliability is somewhat limited due to the technical advances over the last two decades. That being said, modern technologies and increased manufacturing efficiency enables costs to be reduced, so it is likely that this figure would be less given today's infrastructure.

Mars Direct

Robert Zubrin, the creator of the Mars direct/semi-direct mission types, priced the Mars Direct mission between $30-$55 billion. This includes 5 missions over a 20 year period. However, he also noted that it would cost significantly less if executed by a private company [33]. While Zubrin’s claims are estimates, they are useful as ballpark figures. Moreover, since his research underpins NASA’s Design Reference Architecture, he is a voice of authority on the matter.
