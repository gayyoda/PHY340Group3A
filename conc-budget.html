Conclusion: Budget

Without any collaborations, the UKSA is not equipped to execute a Mars mission in a 20 year time frame, and the budget would likely exceed £100 billion. Alternatively, utilizing the private sector, working closely with other space agencies and splitting manufacturing costs would allow for a Mars mission by 2040, likely costing much less than £100 billion.

As such, the feasibility of the proposal relies heavily on the UK’s ability to collaborate. Unfortunately, many useful collaborations may not be offered until the UKSA fully commits to a Mars mission. Due to this, the time related aspect of the mission cannot be backed with certainty. However, a budget of £100 billion should cover the entirety of the mission, regardless of time limitations.
