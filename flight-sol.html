Interplanetary Flight Solution

Radiation

It has been shown that, [30] for astronauts to reach the radiation limit, they would have to spend a year travelling to Mars and over 60 years on its surface. This means that neither short- nor long-term stays are likely to exceed the radiation limits.

Communication

A crew must be extensively briefed before the mission so that they can follow the mission plan and make their own quick decisions if the need arises. This increases the training time required for a given mission.

Dust

Dust is also prevalent on the Martian surface, where dust storms often occur and so there has been a lot of research into how to stop dust from destroying essential equipment. One piece of technology to provide dust shielding is NASA's Electrodynamic Dust Shielding (EDS) [31]. It is already being used in missions on the lunar surface so should be suitable for Martian missions to protect from dust storms.

Power

Batteries, while heavy, are a good solution to the decreasing efficiency of solar panels at greater distance from the Sun. Another potential solution is the use of radiothermal generators (RTGs), which have served as the main power source for many long-range probes. Depending on the amount of radiation shielding around the crew, this may not carry additional radiation-associated risk.
