Selection of Crew

To reduce the effects of spaceflight on the human body, the crew selection should be influenced by their health and fitness levels. At least one member of the crew should also be chosen due to their medical experience [28]. However, having two crew members with medical experience is recommended in case of illness [28].

When choosing the crew, to limit the level of medical challenges when in space and the challenges later in life, especially the risk of radiation-induced cancer, many factors need to be taken into consideration. For example, NASA excludes any candidates that are smokers as they have a higher risk of cancer [16]. It also needs to be taken into consideration that females and younger candidates will have a higher risk of cancer induced by radiation [16].

It is recommended that crew members will need to pass several health criteria before commencing on a deep-space mission. These health criteria include [29]:

  • Healthy kidney function to reduce the risk of kidney stones
  • Low risks of coronary artery disease
  • 20/20 vision (uncorrected or corrected with glasses)
  • No dependency on alcohol, tobacco or drugs
  • A blood pressure that does not exceed 140/90 measured in a sitting position
  • A normal range of motion in all joints
  • Free from any psychiatric disorders

Implementing these conditions should allow for the healthiest crew to be selected.
